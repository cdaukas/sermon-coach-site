You hear "great sermon, pastor" at the door every Sunday.

No one wants to risk offending you. Your wife loves you and tells you what you want to hear. Your elders affirm you with generic encouragement. Your closest friends in ministry are too buried to weigh in on your preaching. The candid, structured feedback that made you grow in homiletics class quietly disappeared once you were the one in the pulpit.

And you've felt it. You can feel the moments you're drifting into moralism, into book-report exposition, into preaching at people instead of TO them.

You don't need another book on preaching. You need an honest read of the sermon you just preached — one that celebrates what's working, names what isn't, and shows you what to sharpen before next Sunday.

That's what The Sermon Coach does.

What It Is

A structured evaluation of your sermon. In minutes, not weeks.

Paste your manuscript or transcript and receive a substantive assessment — scored, narrated, mapped, and prioritized — drawn from expositional homiletical traditions that have shaped the best preaching of the last fifty years.

Who it's for

The Sermon Coach is built for preachers who take the craft seriously but don't have a structured feedback loop. Especially:

  • Pastors in expository evangelical traditions, where the rubric resonates naturally
  • Solo and small-staff churches, where you're the only one preaching most Sundays
  • Preaching cohorts and coaching groups using The Sermon Coach as a shared evaluation tool
  • Seminary preaching courses and homiletics professors
  • Mentors discipling younger preachers

Built By A Pastor For Pastors

Built by a preacher.

The Sermon Coach was built by Dr. Christopher M. Daukas, a former church planter in Phoenix, Arizona, with 25 years of pastoral ministry experience and 14 years as a Lead Pastor. Chris has been preaching for 25 years, has been shaped by these resources, and has spent the last several years sharpening his own preaching against the rubric this tool now offers other preachers.

The Sermon Coach is the actual rubric Chris uses on his own work — including the sample evaluations 1 and 2 above, which he ran on two of his own old sermons. If the rubric is going to find your Fallen Condition Focus drifting, you should know it has already found his.
