"It's clear to me, but I wrote it. How do I know if it will be clear to them?"

The author's curse: fifteen hours in the text and you've read the manuscript so many times you can't see it straight anymore. The most honest fear a preacher carries, and the easiest to miss, because a moralistic sermon still sounds biblical. The Sermon Coach catches the drift while you can still fix it, before three hundred people hear "try harder" in a sermon that was supposed to give them Christ.

What It Is

A structured evaluation of your sermon. In minutes, not weeks.

Paste your manuscript and in a few minutes you get a structured read: scored, narrated, and prioritized, measured against eleven things the expository tradition has always cared about — Chapell, Keller, Piper, Robinson, the Simeon Trust, and 9Marks.

Who it's for

The Sermon Coach is built for preachers who take the craft seriously but don't have a structured feedback loop. Especially:

  • Pastors in expository evangelical traditions, where the rubric resonates naturally
  • Solo and small-staff churches, where you're the only one preaching most Sundays
  • Preaching cohorts and coaching groups using The Sermon Coach as a shared evaluation tool
  • Seminary preaching courses and homiletics professors
  • Mentors discipling younger preachers
