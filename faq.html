Data & Privacy
Should I be worried about my data?
No. Your sermons stay private to your account, are never used to train AI models, and are never shared with third parties. You own your work; The Sermon Coach is just the rubric you run it through.
Who can see my evaluations?
Only you. Each preacher's library is private to their account. Cohort tier members can optionally choose to share specific evaluations with their cohort, but private-by-default is the rule everywhere else.
Can I delete an evaluation?
Yes. You can delete any evaluation, or delete your entire account, at any time. Deletion is permanent.
Will my sermons be used to train AI?
No. The Sermon Coach uses Anthropic's Claude API with data privacy commitments that prohibit using your sermons for model training. Your preaching stays your preaching.
Pricing & Plans
What's included in the Founding Member offer?
50% off for 12 months on whichever tier you choose, plus permanent Founding Member status: priority support direct from Chris, early access to new features, and a Founding Member badge on your account that doesn't expire when your discount does.
What happens after the first 12 months?
Your subscription converts to standard pricing — but your Founding Member status is permanent. You keep priority support, early access, and the badge for as long as you're a subscriber.
Can I switch tiers?
Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately with prorated billing; downgrades take effect at your next billing cycle.
What if I hit my monthly evaluation limit?
You'll see a prompt to upgrade to the next tier. Limits reset on the first of each month. Unused evaluations don't roll over — the cap is meant to encourage thoughtful selection of which sermons get the rubric treatment, not stockpiling.
Is there a free trial?
Not at this time. The samples on the homepage show exactly what every evaluation looks like, and Founding Member pricing makes the first year low-risk. If you're not sure, email Chris directly and he'll run a concierge evaluation on one of your sermons so you can see the rubric on your own work before deciding.
How It Works
What do I submit — a manuscript or audio?
A manuscript. Paste the text directly or upload a .docx file. The evaluation works on the written sermon, which is how the rubric was designed. If you preach from notes rather than a full manuscript, send what you have and Chris can advise during your first concierge evaluation.
How long does an evaluation take?
A few minutes inside the product, once it's live. Concierge evaluations during pre-launch are returned within 48 hours.
What does the evaluation look like?
A structured dashboard scoring your sermon against 11 criteria drawn from Chapell, Simeon Trust, Piper, 9Marks, Keller, and Robinson — with specific anchors in your sermon text, a band-level summary (Faithful, Strong, Exemplary, etc.), and an affirmation paragraph plus an improvement paragraph at the top. See the samples on the homepage for the actual format.
Will it work for my preaching style?
The rubric draws from a specific Reformed evangelical tradition. It will work best for preachers who already operate inside this stream — expository, gospel-centered, text-driven. Optional rubric variants for other evangelical traditions are on the roadmap.
Cohorts
How does the Cohort tier work?
Cohort gives you 5 seats and 30 sermons per month, pooled across the group. Each member has their own private library by default, with the option to share specific evaluations with the cohort. It's built for staff teams at one church, seminary classmates, mentor-mentee pairs, or peer preaching groups.
Can my cohort see my evaluations automatically?
No. Sharing is opt-in per evaluation. You decide which ones to expose to the group and which ones stay private. This mirrors how preachers already work — share the sermon you want feedback on, keep the rest to yourself.
